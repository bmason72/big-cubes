\section{Introduction} \label{sec:intro}

The goal of the ALMA Wideband Sensitivity Upgrade is to increase the correlated bandwidth of ALMA by at least a factor of two with a goal of a factor of 4 as well as increase the overall sensitivity due to improvements to the entire digital signal chain. \citet{Kepley2024DataProperties} estimated the properties of WSU data by extrapolating from the properties of current observations. These properties were used to estimate an order-of-magnitude size of compute for WSU \citep{Kepley2024DataEstimate}.  These estimates were done for two nominal stages of the WSU: early WSU and later WSU. In early WSU, ATAC was assumed to capable of processing two times the current bandwidth and bands 2, 6, and 8 have been upgraded to four times the bandwidth. In later WSU, ATAC was assumed to be capable of processing four times the current bandwidth and all bands have been upgraded to four times the bandwidth.

Recently AMT has defined four different stages of the WSU [xxx cite AMT memo and IST science priorities memo]: Initial WSU System (IWS), Minimum WSU System (WSU), Goal WSU System (GWS), and Full WSU System (FWS). The MWS and FWS are equivalent to early WSU and later WSU stages in \citet{Kepley2024DataProperties}, respectively.  For IWS, ATAC is assumed to be capable of providing two times the current bandwidth and Band 2 has upgraded to four times the current bandwidth along with at least 36 12\,m antennas. The FWS has Bands 2, 6, 7, and 8 upgraded  to four times the current bandwidth and ATAC upgraded to four times the current bandwidth.

Subsequent to that AMT defined 5 milestones for the WSU project, in order to align documentation for the preliminary design review in 2025. Those most crucial for data properties are milestone 1, milestone 4, and milestone 5. This memo estimates the properties of WSU data produced at M1, M4 and M5 and calculates an order-of-magnitude size of compute.  For further details see \citet{Kepley2024DataProperties} and \citet{Kepley2024DataEstimate}.

\section{Method} \label{sec:method}


We use the same methodology as in \citet{Kepley2024DataProperties} and \citep{Kepley2024DataEstimate}. Below we provide an outline of the additional steps that were necessary to compute the IWS and GWS properties and size of compute.

For the IWS, we assumed that only Band 2 upgraded and the maximum bandwidth provided by ATAC for that receiver was two times the current bandwidth (16~GHz). All other receivers were assumed to  have their current bandwidths.  To calculate the spectral resolution, we use the same methodology as in \citet{Kepley2024DataProperties} with the exception that when the number of channels averaged together is less than the band-dependent value given in Table 2 of [xxxx IST memo], we set the channel average to that value and recalculate the velocity and spectral resolution assuming that minimum channel averaging.

We calculated the number of spectral windows by dividing the receiver bandwidth by our assumed spectral window bandwidth of 2~GHz. For Band 6, the total bandwidth is 11.5~GHz. In that case, we rounded up to 6 spectral windows under the assumption that the full bandwidth should be prioritized. The total number of aggregate channels for this case was calculated by taking the total receiver bandwidth and dividing by the spectral resolution for a particular observations and thus is independent of our assumption of the number of spectral windows.

The integration time was assumed to be 6.144s for all cases in IWS. We note that the IST Science Priority memo recommends that long baseline projects that do not exceed the maximum data rate use shorter integration times of 3.072s (see footnote 4 in that memo). However, we do not attempt to account for this practice in our current estimates.

We assume 47 12\,m antennas for IWS. The minimum number of 12\,m antennas for the start IWS is 36, but we assume that more antennas will be added into the array as the IWS proceeds. The maximum data rates given in Table 2 of [xxx IST Science Priorities memo] assume 50 antennas, but that is because they are focused on the maximum possible data rate. Although the 7\,m array is not included in IWS, we calculate those values as well for informational purposes and present their statistics separately.

For GWS, we assume that Bands 2, 6, 7, and 8 are all upgraded to four times the current bandwidth and that ATAC has been upgraded to four times the current bandwidth. The rest of the assumptions are identical to those used for Early and Later WSU in \citet{Kepley2024DataProperties}.
